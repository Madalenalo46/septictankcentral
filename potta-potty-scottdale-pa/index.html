Frequently Asked Questions

What if the location for the unit is on a slight incline?

Our trained technicians are experts at placement. They will use professional-grade leveling blocks to ensure the unit is stable, secure, and completely level for a safe and comfortable user experience, even if the ground is not perfectly flat.

For a multi-day event, is there anything we can do to help keep the unit fresh between services?

While our initial chemical charge is very effective, promoting good ventilation is key. We recommend propping the door slightly ajar when the unit is not in use (if the location is secure) to encourage air circulation. For larger events, the best solution is to schedule additional servicing visits.

Do you offer standalone hand-washing stations?

Yes, we do. In addition to the sinks included in our Event Pro and luxury trailer models, we offer standalone, two-station hand-washing units. These are highly recommended for any event, especially where food is served, to promote superior hygiene.

Is it possible to place a unit in a location that is far from the street?

Our service trucks need to be able to get reasonably close to the unit's location (typically within 25 feet) to safely pump and service the tank. If you have concerns about a remote placement, please discuss them with us so we can determine the feasibility.

Do you offer sanitation services for personal RVs or camper trailers?

While our primary business is providing our own rental units, we may be able to offer pumping services for RVs or campers depending on location and schedule availability. Please call our office to inquire about this specialized service.

What is your procedure for severe weather, such as high winds or a major storm?

Safety is our top priority. Our units are designed to be stable, but in the event of a severe weather forecast, we recommend placing them against a building or in a sheltered area if possible. For long-term rentals, we can also provide stakes to secure the unit to the ground for added stability.

Scottdale, PA Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Early in the 20th century, Scottdale was the center of the Frick coke interests. It had steel and iron pipe mills, brass and silver works, a casket factory, a large milk-pasteurizing plant, and machine shops; all of the aforementioned are presently defunct. Scottdale is notable for its economic decline from a formerly prosperous coke-town into an archetypal Rust Belt town. Duraloy Technologies, "a supplier of specialty high alloy, centrifugal and static cast components and assemblies" is the last remnant of Scottdale's steel related prosperity.
